📊 Cosmetic Retail Business Analysis Report

(Nykaa Sales Dataset)

---

1. Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of product performance, customer behavior, and sales operations within a cosmetic retail dataset. The objective is to identify key revenue drivers, evaluate customer retention, and assess the effectiveness of pricing and discount strategies.

Key findings indicate that excessive discounting on premium products does not significantly improve sales, customer retention is declining, and seasonal trends impact cosmetic demand. Additionally, revenue is concentrated in specific cities and regions, highlighting opportunities for expansion.

2. Business Objectives

- Analyze product and category performance
- Understand customer acquisition and retention trends

3. Data Overview

The dataset consists of multiple interconnected tables including Orders, Order Items, Products, Customers, Categories, and Date.

Key steps performed:

- Data cleaning and transformation
- Relationship modeling between tables
- Creation of calculated measures (Revenue, AOV, Repeat Customers)

4. Product Performance Analysis

The analysis reveals that while discounts are heavily applied (up to 40%) on premium products, these products do not generate proportional sales volume.

Key Observations:

- High-priced products show lower demand despite discounts
- Mid-range products contribute consistently to revenue
- Discount strategy is not effectively aligned with demand

Business Insight:
Discounting alone does not drive sales; product-market fit and pricing strategy are critical.

5. Customer Behavior & Retention Analysis

Customer analysis highlights a decline in repeat customers over time, indicating weak retention strategies.

Key Observations:

- Repeat customer percentage is decreasing
- New customer acquisition is not translating into loyalty
- Seasonal trend observed:
  - During May–June (monsoon period), makeup product sales decline

Business Insight:
Customer retention and seasonal demand significantly influence revenue stability.

6. Sales & Operational Analysis

Sales performance shows strong geographic concentration and varying channel performance.

Key Observations:

- Cities like Mumbai contribute the highest revenue
- West region dominates overall sales
- Other regions show lower penetration → growth opportunity
- Channel performance varies across platforms

Business Insight:
Geographic and channel-based strategies are essential for scaling the business.

7. Key Findings

- High discounts on premium products do not increase sales significantly
- Customer retention is declining, posing a risk to long-term revenue
- Seasonal factors (monsoon) reduce demand for makeup products
- Revenue is concentrated in limited geographic regions
- Mid-range products outperform high-end products

---

8. Business Recommendations

- Optimize discount strategy:
  Focus on mid-range products rather than heavy discounting on premium items

- Improve customer retention:
  Introduce loyalty programs, personalized offers, and memberships

- Seasonal product strategy:
  Promote waterproof and long-lasting makeup during monsoon

- Expand geographically:
  Target underperforming cities and regions for growth

- Reposition premium products:
  Adjust pricing, marketing, or bundling strategies

---

9. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that sustainable growth in the cosmetic retail sector depends on effective pricing strategies, strong customer retention, and regional expansion. By aligning product strategy with customer behavior and seasonal trends, the business can improve both revenue and customer loyalty.
